UX Case Study

AloGame Platform

A centralized login and payment gateway that allows users to authenticate and manage payments

My Role

Product Designer

Timeline

2023

Team

Summary

We identified a decline in Monthly Active Users (MAU) and low Customer Satisfaction (CSAT) in the login-to-payment flow. By analyzing the user journey, we discovered that high drop-off rates occurred at the login step, particularly for users managing multiple game accounts. Our solution introduced a streamlined login experience, reducing friction and improving drop-off rate, CSAT, and returning users, leading to increased MAU.

About Project

Product Overview

Alogame is a centralized login and payment gateway that allows users to authenticate and manage payments across multiple game platforms. Users can log into different game portals, deposit/withdraw funds, and switch between platforms seamlessly within Alogame.

The Role

This project was an extension of Alopay, where I worked closely with Giang, the Product Owner, from wireframing to final UI solutions. I facilitated kickoff and handoff sessions with the development team while encouraging developer participation in product iterations to ensure seamless execution.

Background

The initial version required users to return to the homepage every time they needed to log in to a different game portal or if their session expired. This added unnecessary steps, causing frustration, especially for users playing multiple games.

After choosing a portal to login.

The Problems

  • High drop-off rates at the login step, preventing users from reaching the payment stage.

  • Low CSAT scores, indicating frustration with repeated logins.

  • Fewer returning users, leading to lower MAU and higher churn rate.

User Research & Insights

By analyzing user journeys and drop-off points, we found that:

  • Many users play on multiple game platforms and frequently switch between them.
  • The requirement to navigate back to the homepage made the process tedious.
  • The added effort resulted in delays in payment completion and user drop-offs.

The Solutions

Optimizing the Login Experience

To address these pain points, we redesigned the login experience with a focus on reducing friction and improving efficiency:

1. Dropdown Login Selector:

Instead of forcing users back to the homepage, we introduced a dropdown in the "Sign in to X" section, enabling seamless platform switching. To enhance usability, we incorporated provider logos, aligning with the "Recognize rather than recall" heuristic for a more intuitive experience.

Quick Login Selector.

2. One-Click Re-Login:

If the session was still active, users could seamlessly log back in without entering credentials again.

To those who have logged in some portals before.

These improvements significantly reduced time on task for logging in, making it easier for users to reach the payment stage without frustration.

The Results

Outcomes

By simplifying login, drop-off dropped from 40% to 20%, boosting completed transactions. Easier portal switching increased returning users, driving MAU up by 15%. A smoother login flow led to CSAT of the payment flow rising to 4.6/5

Take aways
  • Reducing friction at login directly improves retention and payments.
  • A small UX change (dropdown login switcher) significantly enhanced user experience.

By optimizing the login flow, we improved user engagement and made Alogame a seamless multi-platform gateway, ultimately driving better conversion rates.
